About Me

Hey There, I'm Wesley

Thanks for checking out my website! I'm currently a college student at Columbia University, where I study Electrical Engineering and Computer Science.

My journey's taken me around the world, from my birthplace in California to where I grew up in Tokyo and Jakarta to where I currently live in New York City.

While moving around across Asia, I discovered a passion for creating things that began with intricate 2D tile structures and Legos but developed into a love for programming and robotics.

I first stumbled into programming when I logged onto Scratch for the first time. Before I knew it, I was spending hours everyday on the site and by the time I left the platform, I had made over 100 projects, some of which have endured to this day.

This captivating introduction to programming led me to dive deeper into the world of software, where I now control robots in Java, develop automation tools in Python, and program satellites in C++.

The other key defining moment in my life was when I discovered robotics through the VEX Robotics Competition in middle school. Here, I discovered a new obsession with robotics and would go on to sink countless hours in the school's makeshift robotics lab, every chance I got.

Entering high school, I carried my love for robotics with me and co-founded a FIRST Robotics Competition team, where I designed and built a robot every year with other passionate teammates.

Since then, I've continued to work on robotics and explore a ton of fascinating projects that you can check out here!
